ETHICCRAFT CLUB MEMBER

Ethiccraft members are the youths who are connected with the club activities at some level. These may include youths from colleges, coaching or professional background. Members of Ethiccraft Club are expected to volunteer for the different programs conducted by club in order to ensure smooth conduct of the events and also help in connecting people with the club to bless with the opportunity of being instrumental in bringing a positive transformation. Some of the responsibilities of club members are as follows:

  • Taking responsibility of connecting people to values through Ethiccraft Club.
  • Enhancing their In-build talents and skills and employ them in various activities conducted to spread values by club.
  • Volunteer for the club responsibilities and leadership for spreading values.
  • Help to spread awareness among youth about club by connecting them to various value development, educational seminar and social welfare activities of Ethiccraft Club.
  • Become instrumental in training and engaging the new members as per their nature and talents.

Club Membership Standards are as follows:

Membership Responsibilities

What the people get out of Ethiccraft Club depends largely on what they put into it. Many membership requirements are designed to help club members more fully participate in and enjoy a life-transforming experience.

1. Attendance

Attending weekly club meetings allows members to enjoy fellowship, enrich their professional and personal knowledge and meet other business leaders in their community. Club members are encouraged to attend all meetings and to make-up at other branches of the club throughout the Delhi-NCR when they are unable to attend their regular club meeting. Members are required to attend 50% of the meetings in each six-month period and not miss four consecutive meetings in order to remain an active member. A member can make-up the meeting they missed within a two-week window prior to or after the club meeting they missed.

2. Service

By participating in local and national service projects, club members can volunteer their time and talents where they’re most needed. The five Avenues of Service are philosophical cornerstone and the foundation on which club activity is based:

  1. CLUB SERVICE: focuses on strengthening fellowship and ensuring the club’s effective functioning. VOCATIONAL SERVICE encourages club members to serve others through their vocations and to practice high ethical standards.
  2. COMMUNITY SERVICE: covers the projects and activities the club undertakes to improve life in its community.
  3. INTERNATIONAL SERVICE: encompasses actions taken to expand club’s humanitarian reach around the globe and to promote world understanding and peace.
  4. NEW GENERATIONS: recognizes the positive change implemented by youth and young adults through leadership development activities, involvement in community and international service projects, and exchange programs that enrich and foster world peace and cultural understanding.

3. Finding and keeping members

To keep clubs strong, every club member must share the responsibility of bringing new people into club. Even new members can bring guests to club meetings or invite them to participate in a service project. Keeping members involved in club is another responsibility. Fostering strong fellowship and encouraging early participation in service projects are two of the best ways to sustain a club’s membership.

4. Leadership

Club members are encouraged to volunteer for leadership roles at the club level and beyond. Leadership opportunities include becoming a chair or a member of a club committee.
